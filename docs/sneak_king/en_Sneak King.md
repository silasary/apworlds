# Sneak King

## Where is the options page?

The [player options page for this game](../player-options) contains all the options you need to configure and
export a config file.

## What does randomization do to this game?

The four levels (Sawmill, Cul-De-Sac, Construction, Downtown) are treated as an open world. Each level's 20 missions
are locked behind mission unlock items, and levels themselves may require unlock items or a completion count in a
previous level before they become accessible.

## What is the goal of Sneak King when randomized?

Complete a set number of missions or A-rank completions across all levels, depending on your chosen goal option.

- **Complete X Missions** — complete the required number of missions at any rank across all levels.
- **Complete X A-Ranks** — earn A-rank on the required number of missions across all levels.

The goal count is configured with the Goal Range option (default 20, max 80).

## What items and locations can get shuffled?

Locations:
- All 20 missions per level at each enabled rank (C, B, A) — up to 240 mission locations total
- Interactable objects across all four levels (hiding spots, doors, ladders, etc.)

Items:
- Mission unlock items for each level (e.g. "Sawmill Mission 5 Unlock")
- Level unlock items when using the unlock item method (e.g. "Sawmill Unlock")

## What does another world's item look like in Sneak King?

Receiving items from other worlds happens automatically in the background — no in-game visual change occurs.

## When the player receives an item, what happens?

Mission unlock items make the corresponding mission scroll visible in-game. Level unlock items make the first mission
of that level available.
