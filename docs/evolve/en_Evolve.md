# Evolve

## What is this game?

**Evolve** is a randomizer for the game *Evolve Incremental*. This game, by Demagorddon, is all about creating your civilzilation and continually evolving towards the best possible. You start out as single celled organism, beccome multicelled, create a city, and onwards.

If you find any bugs, or have any suggestions, please open an issue on this page!

## ***I DO NOT RECOMMEND THIS TO BE YOUR FIRST PLAY THROUGH OF EVOLVE!!! It will probally not be very fun (i dont know but i dont recommend it)***

The goal for this game is a Tier 1 Reset, or Mutually Assured Destruction.

## Where is the options page?

config file. This is only availible when you download the .apworld file and use the Archipelago Client options generator.

## What is randomized?

Yea, this was the hardest part. There really isn't any "randomization", except for the CRISPR ig.

You do start with CRISPR ancients 1 and 2.

## What locations get shuffled?

These are the locations to unlock further items:
- The entire tech tree from Primitive to Globalized
- Building at least one of each bulding

## What items get shuffled?

The item pool includes:
- I will be adding some of the more important techs
- You can get random CRISPR upgrades
  - creep 1 - 5
  - store 1 - 4
  - enhance 1
  - birth 1
  - crafty 1 - 3
  - queue 2
  - governor 1 - 3
  - trader 1 - 2
  - ancients 3 - 6
  - transcendence 1

Filler Items:
- Build a building   (16.7%)
- Get some resources   (33.3%)
- Prestige:   (16.7%)
  - Plasmids:   (8.3%)
    - Get 1 - 10   (4.2%)
    - Get 10 - 50   (2.4%)
    - Get 50 - 100   (1.2%)
    - Get 100 - 200   (0.6%)
  - Phage:   (5.0%)
    - Get 1 - 10   (1.9%)
    - Get 10 - 25   (1.6%)
    - Get 25 - 50   (0.8%)
  - Anti-Plasmids:   (3.3%)
    - Get 1 - 10   (2.5%)
    - Get 10 - 20   (0.8%)
- +5MW Power (is broke) (8.3%)
- +5% Production (8.3%)
- +1 Population (8.3%)
- Traps: (8.3%)
  - Lose resources (3.6%)
  - A rival city attacks (2.4%)
  - -1MW Power (1.2%)
  - -2% Production (1.2%)


Filler items can appear often (and do) because there are not a lot of items, unfortunately.
