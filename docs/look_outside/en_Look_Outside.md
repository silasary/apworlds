# Look Outside

From the game's steam description: Look Outside is a survival horror RPG set in a single apartment building. A mysterious event turns anyone who looks out the window into grotesque monsters, leaving the world in absolute chaos. Scavenge the building to seek food, supplies, and weapons while encountering strange characters - human and otherwise.

It is heavily discouraged that you play this randomizer before playing the vanilla game. There's a lot that requires context to understand here. Massive plot spoilers below:

## How the randomizer works

Look Outside is a very large randomizer, with 1,000 locations +. Each enemy encounter is unique, and has been randomized as a location. Every overworld item as well as quest reward has also been randomized.

The vanilla game has a time limit, a lot of branching quest paths, and mutually exclusive content. This randomizer mod has been adjusted to allow the player to achieve everything on a single save file.

The same locations will be available on all difficulties. This means that enemies that are ordinarily only available on Cursed difficulty will appear even if the player started their run on Survivor or Explorer. The same goes for overworld items that are normally restricted to appearing on only Explorer, Survivor, or Cursed difficulties: all three types will appear in all difficulties.

If the same enemy has multiple forms depending on the difficulty, the form they appear in when engaged with in combat will still be dependent on the chosen difficulty.

# Quality of life changes

Multiple QOL adjustments have been made so everything can be done on the same save file:

A new option on the pause menu, “Go Home”, will allow the player to return to their apartment from anywhere, even the roof. If the player lets all four astronomers mutate and their head starts splitting open, it will be like that for the rest of the playthrough, but their walk speed will be returned to the default.

Location checks that remove an item from the player's inventory that could be used in other contexts will no longer remove said item.

- Placing Cinnamon the guinea pig on the chair in the Security room will not remove her from your inventory. The tape the player used to record on will not be changed.

- Exposing the void disc to the light from Edwin's telescope will not remove it from the player's inventory.

- Using the negative disc to expose the photo paper in Vincent's apartment will not remove the photo paper from the player's inventory.

- Giving the exposed paper to Lyle or developing it in Lyle's darkroom will not remove it from the player's inventory.

All offering location checks will only allow the player to take the actions that would result in a correct offering. The player is prevented from exposing the photo paper to anything but the negative disc, recording any footage except for the channel that mutated cinnamon, or picking up any painting but the correct one.

Any recruitment or location checks that depend on reaching a certain disposition with a character or are missable with the wrong dialogue have been adjusted.

- The secret door at the bottom of the stairwell will no longer vanish when interacted with

- The Spider will attack the player no matter what they say during the interview.

- Joel will accept the player’s recruitment offer no matter what they said during his initial dialogue.

- The player gets the same location check from Ben, no matter how good of a playmate they were.

- Audrey will accept the player’s recruitment offer no matter what they said during her quest dialogues. If the player recruits Audrey before finding the note from Mutt on her back, then they will be able to get the vending machine key location from Mutt afterwards.

- Nestor will always leave to go on a date with Rafta even if the player doesn’t encourage him after giving him the letter. The player cannot give him the love letter until they have given the stationery and pen to Rafta.

- Eugene will continue to restock his shop, even after being possessed by Nestor.

- If the player makes the wrong dialogue choice during the encounter with the spider husk, they can restart the conversation by leaving and re-initiating.

- The player will be able to summon any door encounter that they don’t yet have the location check for from the cheat event near their front door. The player will always be able to summon any merchant encounter, as well as Hobbs the cook, even after killing them.

- The clock in the player’s living room has been turned into a spot where the player can set the current time of day.

- The further mutated forms of every tooth apartment family member will spawn on the appropriate days, even if their previous forms have been killed previously.

- Locations are granted based on the number of total Freds killed in the Fred apartment rather than for each individual Freds that has been killed. Rewards for leaving the last Fred alive are no longer granted, save for the cash bribe from Godhead Fred and the item duplication from the true Fred.

- The calendar in the player’s bedroom has been turned into a spot where they can set the current day or undo actions that could block them from locations, through the “Ease regrets.” option. A complete list of regrets is below:

- Sybil.

- Wilhelmina.

# In-game Events

Since the player can accomplish things out of order, some in-game events may have unclear trigger requirements. Here are some examples:

- The player can start the blackout event at any time by interacting with the lamp in their bedroom after they have been granted the “Elevator Activation” item. The blackout can be ended prematurely by interacting with the lamp again.

- Jeanne's final stage of mutation, Harriet and Sophie's return to their apartment, and Ernest's kidnapping are all triggered by entering the ground floor through the planet-locked door through the stairwell. Entering the ground floor through the elevator will have no effect. (This is also the case in the vanilla game, though not seen because in vanilla it is impossible to use the elevator before entering the ground floor through the stairwell)
