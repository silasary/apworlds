# Metroid: Samus Returns

## What does randomization do to this game?

Items the player would normally acquire throughout the game have been shuffled. In addition to the
suit upgrades and expansion pickups, the strands of DNA normally acquired from eliminating the
Metroids and the hatchling Metroid are also included in the shuffle. The amiibo reserve tanks can
also be shuffled into the game. The shuffle includes logic so that the game can always be completed.

In order to make the world more open, the pools of purple liquid that would normally require Samus
to eliminate the Metroids no longer need to be drained, and you are no longer closed off from
accessing your ship once you venture into the caverns of SR388.

In order to make the experience more seamless, the text box that normally appears when collecting a
pickup now displays an onscreen message without pausing the game, and a feature has been added to
allow you to warp to your starting location at Save Stations.

## What is the goal of *Metroid: Samus Returns* when randomized?

The end goal of the randomized game consists of:

* Collecting the required amount of Metroid DNA
* Locating the hatchling Metroid
* Defeating Proteus Ridley

## Which items can be in another player's world?

All shuffled items can be in another player's world. However, you can configure the amount of DNA
that must be somewhere in your own world.

## What does another world's item look like in *Metroid: Samus Returns?*

Items from the other games in the *Metroid* series appear as themselves if it has a counterpart in
*Samus Returns.* Some items from the other games that don't exist in the original *Samus Returns*
also have graphics when shuffled into the randomized game.

All other items appear as 'RDV' logos if they are progression items, and item spheres ("nothing")
otherwise.

## What other differences are there from the original game?

The randomizer makes several changes from the unmodified game to make the area traversal work better
with random items.

General changes:
* Because the hazardous liquid starts drained, the Chozo seals have been repurposed to provide
hints. Additional Chozo seals have been added to provide extra hints.
* You can warp to your starting room by cancelling the save prompt at a Save Station or the gunship.
* Gamma Metroids always spawn in the same arena and no longer flee after taking enough damage.
* Power Bomb drop rates from enemies have been improved.
* Heated rooms and lava deal less damage to Samus and no longer deal more damage the longer Samus
stays in them.
* Scan Pulse no longer uses Aeion (although you need to have at least 1 point to activate it)
* Beam doors (including Charge) cannot be opened with Power Bombs.

World changes:
* The versions of the surface from the original game are two different maps in the unmodified game.
In the randomizer, you can freely traverse between the two areas by going through the crystal
blocks in Landing Site until you have the DNA requirement to finish the game.
* Most cutscenes are removed.
* The Diggernaut chase sequence has been skipped.
* Many doors have been adjusted to make traversal easier. Most notably, several Charge Beam doors
leading to the next area were changed to normal doors, and several one-way doors are now two-way.
* Most of the grapple blocks in the exits to elevator rooms have been removed.
* Pitfall blocks in several places in the surface and area 1 are changed to beam blocks.

Optional changes:
* Collecting a tank can refill that ammo to max capacity. In the unmodified game, only energy and
Aeion are fully refilled when collecting a tank.
